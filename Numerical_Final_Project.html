1. Introduction
In numerical computations of complex dierential equations, Finite-Dierence
methods in general are used extensively to create sets of mesh points at which
derivatives are approximated and the particular initial or boundary value data are
imposed. Then systems of equations are solved to satisfy the dierential equation.
This paper presents a derivation and overview of dierence methods for linear and
non-linear, second order ordinary dierential equations. With numerical solutions
to partial dierential equations being the motivation for this project, discussion
of application of nite-dierence methods to the wave equation will serve as a
conclusion.
2. 11.3 Finite-Difference Methods for Linear Problems
In comparison with earlier methods of dierential equation approximation in
Chapter 11 of our text, nite-dierence methods for linear problems have better sta-
bility characteristics, but require more computation to obtain a specied accuracy.
For boundary-value problems(BVP), nite dierence methods replace each deriv-
ative in the ODE with the appropriate dierence quotient approximation, many
of which were discussed in chapter 4 including the three and ve point- midpoint
formulas for rst and second derivatives.
Depending on the equation, the computational equipment, or the physical phe-
nomena being modelled, the dierence quotient and step size are chosen to maintain
a certain order of truncation error. Due to the the instability of derivative approxi-
mations resulting from the dominance of round-o error for small step size h values,
our choice of h should not be excessively small.
